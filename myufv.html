myUFV Redesigned - Case Study

Introduction

The redesign of myUFV was a project undertaken by the IT department at UFV (University of the Fraser Valley). As a Communications Specialist Co-op student within the department, I played a pivotal role in facilitating communication between the project team and the students. This case study outlines the methods employed, tools utilized, and the overall success of the project.

July 2023 to September 2023

I was involved in the communication part of the project, mainly communicating the changes and new features of myUFV among UFV students.

I was responsible for creating a survey for students about the changes in the design and new features of the myUFV portal. I was also responsible for communicating this survey and changes happening in the portal through social media and web.

I mainly worked with my boss and a Business Analyst during this project:

  • Kristi Wood: Director of Client Experience
  • Michael Levesque: Business Analyst

Project Summary

The goal of the project was to redesign myUFV, the UFV’s student and employee portal, to improve user experience and incorporate new features. This initiative aimed to enhance student engagement with the platform and streamline access to essential services and information.


Goal

The main goal for my part of the role was to create and communicate the survey to gain student feedback on the new design and features of the myUFV portal. It also involved communicating changes through social media.

As my position changed in September to IT Knowledge Management Analyst, my goal was to update all the myUFV knowledge base articles according to the new myUFV design and features.

It was a major project as it was impacting all the UFV students. It also impacted employees, faculty, and staff, as they also use myUFV.


Challenge

One of the challenges of the myUFV redesign project was effectively communicating the changes and gathering feedback from all the students. As a Communications Specialist Co-op student, I had to ensure that the communication regarding the redesign was clear, concise, and reached a wide audience.

Additionally, coordinating the distribution of communication materials and promoting the survey across various channels required careful planning and execution. Ensuring high participation in the survey to gather meaningful feedback posed another challenge, as student engagement can vary based on factors such as timing and awareness of the initiative. Overall, overcoming these communication and engagement challenges were crucial to the success of the project.


Strategy

Colors used: white and shades of green (colors of UFV)

Tools used:

  • Microsoft Forms: Employed in creating the survey to gather feedback from students regarding the myUFV redesign.
  • Canva: Used for designing communication and marketing materials such as posters, bookmarks, and social media posts.

myUFV Redesigned Logo


Creation of the survey

To gather feedback on the proposed design and features, a survey was created using Microsoft Forms. The survey included questions aimed at understanding students’ preferences and expectations regarding the myUFV redesign. The questions were both about the old and the new design of myUFV.


Communication Material Design

Using Canva, various communication materials were designed to promote the survey and inform students about the upcoming changes to myUFV. This survey was communicated through the following sources of communication:

  • Instagram
  • Twitter
  • UFV Student Newsletter
  • Bookmark
  • Poster

Survey Promotion

The designed materials were strategically distributed across campus and online platforms to encourage student participation in the survey. Social media posts were scheduled to reach a wider audience and generate awareness about the redesign project.


Post-Redesign Communication

Following the launch of the new myUFV, additional Instagram posts were created to highlight the changes and new features. These posts served to educate students about the revamped platform and encourage its adoption.

Knowledge Base Articles

I was also involved in creating and editing ITS knowledge base articles that contained information about myUFV. Here is the link to the myUFV category in the ITS Knowledge Base itservicedesk.ufv.ca. I was working on around 10 – 15 articles about myUFV.


Project Outcome

The myUFV redesign project was deemed successful, with the changes effectively communicated to the student body. The survey received participation of 107 students, providing valuable insights into student preferences. The communication materials created facilitated widespread awareness and generated excitement about the upcoming changes. Additionally, the post-redesign communication efforts helped students to adapt to the new platform seamlessly.


Conclusion

The myUFV redesign project demonstrated the importance of effective communication in driving successful initiatives within an organization. Through the strategic use of surveys, social media, and other communication channels, the project team was able to engage students and ensure a smooth transition to the revamped platform. This experience not only contributed to the enhancement of myUFV but also highlighted the value of collaborated efforts in achieving organizational goals.
